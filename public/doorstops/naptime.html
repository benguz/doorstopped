Bring Back Nap Time

From taking a break yourself to creating an ~official~ school nap policy.

Start small. If you're tired, ask a teacher to take a nap in their classroom during lunch.

Normalize. Make it socially acceptable to ask a teacher that, and make sleep part of your brand. 

  • Respond to “Uggghhh I got four hours of sleep” with, “Homie, I just slept for DAYS in Mrs. Frizzle’s room.”
  • Split a MASSIVE fluffy $20 blanket with your friends, then share it. Invite other tired-looking people to use it. 
  • Remember begging your mom for a dog? Channel that, bestie. Beg all your teachers to buy blankets for their classroom.
  • Gas people up for sleeping during lunch
  • Find spaces in the school that are comfy and usually empty (drama classroom, library). Start a sleep club that meets during lunch in these spaces and get a teacher supervisor.

Negotiate. Commit to helping other students sleep better at home if the school approves the use of advisory time as nap time. Win-win. Email your principal about it! Or ask for this anonymously here.

Here’s two strategies you can give your friends:

1. If you have insomnia (can’t fall asleep / anxious about sleep) and can’t/won’t seek medical care, download the CBT-i Coach app. CBT-i is a proven strategy for treating insomnia, and it even works online without a doctor.

You’re getting more sleep than you think and losing one night’s sleep isn’t that bad—you don’t have to be anxious. Find something calming and when you have intrusive anxious thoughts, ask yourself: what’s the worst that can happen?

2. If you’re using your phone a lot before bed, don’t try to cut it out all at once: try to use slightly less addictive apps instead, getting you to bed a little earlier because, well, you get bored, then move down the ladder every week, eliminating one app at a time.
